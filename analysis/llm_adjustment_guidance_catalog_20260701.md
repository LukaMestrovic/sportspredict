## Research Basis

- Direct odds and de-vigged probabilities: bookmaker odds embed implied probability and margin; use coherent same-book outcome sets where possible. Sources: [Investopedia implied probability and vig](https://www.investopedia.com/articles/dictionary/042215/understand-math-behind-betting-odds-gambling.asp), [Elaad, Reade and Singleton on bookmaker market information](https://www.reading.ac.uk/web/files/economics/emdp201910.pdf).
- Contract timing: 2026 World Cup hydration breaks are three minutes in each half at about 22 minutes into the half; IFAB stoppage time explicitly includes substitutions, disciplinary sanctions, VAR, celebrations, and drinks/cooling breaks. Sources: [FIFA hydration breaks](https://inside.fifa.com/organisation/news/hydration-breaks-world-cup-2026-player-welfare), [IFAB Law 7](https://www.theifab.com/laws/latest/the-duration-of-the-match/).
- Goals and shots: goals are time-skewed rather than uniform; shot quality depends heavily on distance, angle, defenders, and body part; Opta defines SOT as deliberate attempts on target, goals, saves, and last-line blocks. Sources: [Temporal dynamics of goal scoring](https://arxiv.org/html/2501.18606v1), [Soccermatics shooting geometry](https://soccermatics.readthedocs.io/en/latest/lesson2/GeometryOfShooting.html), [Opta definitions](https://theanalyst.com/articles/opta-football-stats-definitions).
- Player props: role, usage, expected minutes, lineup confirmation, set pieces, penalties, and matchup quality drive goals, assists, shots, and cards. Sources: [World Cup player props guide](https://www.mybookie.ag/sports-betting-guide/world-cup-player-props-explained/), [Covers World Cup player props](https://www.covers.com/world-cup/lamine-yamal-odds-props), [set-piece goals and assists](https://www.expectinggoals.com/p/predicting-set-piece-goals-and-assists).
- Cards and fouls: referee tendencies, team style, pressing/duels, match importance, fatigue, and knockout tension matter. Sources: [yellow-card betting factors](https://statshub.com/blog/yellow-card-betting), [fouls betting factors](https://www.squawka.com/en/bet/football-tips/fouls-tips/), [IFAB Law 12](https://www.thefa.com/football-rules-governance/lawsandrules/laws/football-11-11/law-12---fouls-and-misconduct).
- Corners and offsides: corners depend on width, crosses, blocked shots, territory, and chase states; offsides depend on defensive-line height, timing of runs, and through-ball style. Sources: [corner betting factors](https://playthepercentage.com/blog/corner-betting-our-thoughts-and-techniques), [corner market mechanics](https://statsbet.org/football/predictions/corners), [high backline/offside trap](https://the-footballanalyst.com/defending-with-a-high-backline-football-tactics-explained/).
- Goal methods: headers depend on aerial/set-piece/crossing matchups; outside-box goals remain low-probability and shot-geometry driven. Sources: [StatsBomb set-piece aerial analysis](https://blogarchive.statsbomb.com/articles/soccer/using-advanced-data-to-inform-set-piece-strategy/), [StatsBomb goal-chain analysis](https://blogarchive.statsbomb.com/articles/soccer/3781-ways-to-score-a-goal/).
- Substitutions: the local all-history artifact spans 2015-2026, so substitution markets mix pre- and post-five-substitution eras. IFAB introduced the five-substitute option temporarily in 2020 and made it permanent in the 2022/23 Laws; current Law 3 allows up to five substitutes in official competitions, with three opportunities plus half-time. Sources: [IFAB permanent five-sub option](https://www.theifab.com/news/the-ifab-permanently-approves-five-substitute-option-in-top-level-competitions/), [IFAB Law 3](https://www.theifab.com/laws/latest/the-players/).
